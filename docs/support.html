How to Use the App

REIR Calculator is designed to be straightforward. Here is the general workflow:

  1. Select an Organ at Risk (OAR) from the list at the top.
  2. Enter the Total Dose and Number of Fractions for Course 1 and Course 2. The dose per fraction is calculated automatically.
  3. Optionally enable Time-Based Reduction for one or both courses and select the time interval between treatments.
  4. Tap CALCULATE EQD2 to compute the cumulative EQD2 and remaining allowable dose for the selected OAR.
  5. In the Allowed Prescribed Dose panel, choose your desired number of fractions and tap CALCULATE DOSE to see the total dose and dose per fraction for the new course.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EQD2?

EQD2 (Equivalent Dose in 2 Gy fractions) is a radiobiological metric used to normalize radiation doses given in different fractionation schemes to a common scale, based on the linear-quadratic model.

What does the α/β ratio represent?

The α/β ratio is a tissue-specific parameter in the linear-quadratic model that describes a tissue's sensitivity to fraction size. Late-responding tissues (like the spinal cord) typically have a low α/β (~2–3 Gy), while tumors and early-responding tissues have a higher α/β (~10 Gy).

Why is the CALCULATE EQD2 button disabled?

At least one course must have a valid Total Dose and Number of Fractions entered before the calculation can proceed.

What do the OAR tolerance limits represent?

The tolerance limits shown are based on AAPM/ASTRO re-irradiation guidelines and represent the cumulative EQD2 considered acceptable for each organ. These are reference values for educational purposes only.

Does the app save my data?

No. All data is cleared when you close the app or press CLEAR. Nothing is stored or transmitted.
